Curitiba, Brazil

Known as the green capital of Brazil, the city of Curitiba is renowned for its dedicated policies that prioritzes the environment. Located in the southern state of Paraná, the city has a population of nearly 4 million inhabitants called Curitibanos. Since the 1970s politicans have been working to improve the urban infrastructure to accomodate the growing population. Leaders such as Jaime Lerner, who was the former mayor of Curitiba, brainstormed new ideas to help the city develop. Lerner often collaborated with the Curitiba Research and Urban Planning Institute (IPPUC). In 1974 the city launched it’s Bus Rapid Transit System with the help of the IPPUC. This helped organize and reduce traffic in the city. It also became a cheaper and quicker mode of transportation for residents, thus being a feasible option to cars. This lessens the need for automobiles which is important for the environment because it reduces CO2 emissions. Most of the people rely on public transportation, 85% of Curitibanos use the bus system.
